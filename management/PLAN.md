## Strategic Context

### Core Hypothesis
Disorder influences femtosecond-scale nonlinear optical response primarily by modifying the **transport** of photoinjected carriers, not the injection process itself. The orthogonal pump-probe geometry isolates this transport regime. Observable effects are captured by a Drude model with time-dependent effective mass $m^*(t) \propto A^4(t)$, where disorder:
- Increases momentum relaxation rate $\gamma$
- Reduces the *variation* in effective mass required to fit data (due to more uniform conduction band population in amorphous structures)
- Smoothes and broadens the DOS

This framework applies to both weakly disordered crystals and fully amorphous silicon, suggesting a continuous, rather than qualitatively distinct, evolution of transport physics with disorder.

### Key Uncertainties
*   **Dipole Signature (Critical, resolution imminent):** Does disorder leave a distinct qualitative signature in the dipole oscillations (FID) beyond generic ensemble dephasing? If no clear signal emerges, this line will be dropped from the thesis to conserve time.
*   **Microscopic Origin (Open):** Can the observed DOS smoothing/broadening quantitatively predict the transport model parameters ($\alpha$, $m^*$)? Or is the connection only qualitative?
*   **Energy Distribution (Open):** Does disorder alter the energy distribution of photoinjected carriers? This would provide a critical missing link between DOS changes and transport observations.
*   **Photoinjection Dynamics (Low priority):** Initial time-domain investigations showed no qualitative differences. Energy-domain analysis may reveal subtle effects, but this is not critical for the thesis narrative.
